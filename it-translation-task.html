Shared Task: Machine Translation of IT domain

The focus of this task is a domain adaptation of MT to the IT domain and translation of questions and answers. The motivation is a cross-lingual help service, where user questions (troubleshooting for both hardware and software) are translated from Portuguese, Spanish, Czech, Dutch and German into English, and the answers are translated from English back to the users' languages.

The questions usually consist of one sentence, but rarely more, e.g. "I cannot receive e-mails. I think my IP's been blacklisted. What should I do?" and they contain authentic (noisy, informal) language of the users. The answers are generally longer than the questions (usually one sentence, but sometimes more).

You may participate in any or all of the following language pairs:

  • German-English (questions)
  • English-German (answers)
  • Czech-English (questions)
  • English-Czech (answers)
  • Spanish-English (questions)
  • English-Spanish (answers)
  • Portuguese-English (questions)
  • English-Portuguese (answers)
  • Dutch-English (questions)
  • English-Dutch (answers)

DATA FOR DOWNLOAD

Europarl corpus, CzEng (for en-cs, avoid sections 98 and 99), any monolingual sets from WMT16 or previous years
in-domain training data
Batch1 (1000 questions and 1000 answers)
in-domain development data
Batch2 (1000 questions and 1000 answers)

TEST SET SUBMISSION

Unlike in the News translation task, punctuation in the official test sets will not be altered.

To submit your results, please first convert into into SGML format as required by the NIST BLEU scorer, and then upload it to the website matrix.statmt.org.
